Navigation clarity
1. Rename “Mental Health Service”
Clients skim navigation quickly. “Mental Health Service” is vague and doesn’t signal what they’ll find inside. From your current page content :

Current: Mental Health Service

Clearer options:

Services

Therapy Services

What I Offer

Therapy & Specialties

These options match what clients expect and reduce hesitation about where to click.

🔐 Add a short explanation of the Client Portal
Your site currently lists Client Portal in the top navigation with no context. From your current page content :

Add a one‑line explanation on the homepage or under the button:

“Access your secure portal for scheduling, paperwork, and messaging.”

“Log in to complete forms, manage appointments, and communicate securely.”

This prevents clients from wondering whether the portal is for new clients, existing clients, or both.

🗂️ Grouping services into clearer categories
Your services are currently presented as one long list, which can feel dense for clients skimming for relevance. From your current page content :

Current list includes:

Individual Therapy

Couples and Family Therapy

Co‑occurring Disorders Treatment

Trauma‑Informed Care

Crisis Assessment

Supervision & Consultation

Clearer grouping example:

Individual & Relational Therapy
Individual Therapy

Couples and Family Therapy

Specialized Clinical Services
Co‑occurring Disorders Treatment

Trauma‑Informed Care

Crisis Assessment

Professional Services
Supervision & Consultation

This structure helps clients quickly find what applies to them without reading every paragraph.

💵 Clarify the fee structure
Your fee section is clear, but clients may skim past the OON explanation because it’s dense. From your current page content :

Current OON language: “An itemized statement (superbill) can be provided upon request to submit to your insurer for possible reimbursement…”

Clearer, client‑friendly version: “You can request a superbill to submit to your insurance for possible reimbursement. Your insurance plan determines whether they will reimburse you and at what amount.”

This keeps the legal accuracy but reduces cognitive load.

❓ FAQ clarity
Two small adjustments would make the FAQ easier to skim. From your current page content :

1. Avoid repeating the About page
Current FAQ question: “Who is my therapist and what is her approach?”

Clearer alternative: “What is your therapeutic approach?” (Then link to the About page for your bio.)

2. Make confidentiality more concrete
Current: “Clinical records are confidential and protected by law…”

Clearer: “Your information is confidential and protected by law. The only exceptions are situations involving immediate safety concerns or legal requirements, which we review together during intake.”

This grounds the concept in something clients can picture.
